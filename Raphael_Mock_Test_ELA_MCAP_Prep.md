## 📖 **PART 1: READING COMPREHENSION** (20 points)

### **Passage: "The Jazz Unit Adventure"**  
*(Based on email mentioning new Jazz unit beginning)*

Students in Mr. McClure's class were excited to begin their new unit on Jazz music. As part of their preparation for the upcoming MCAP tests, they would explore the rich history of this American art form. Jazz originated in the late 19th and early 20th centuries in African American communities, particularly in New Orleans. 

The music combines elements of blues, ragtime, and European harmonic structure. What makes Jazz special is its emphasis on improvisation - where musicians create melodies on the spot, often building on a familiar tune. Famous early jazz musicians like Louis Armstrong and Duke Ellington helped make Jazz popular across the United States and around the world.

As part of their unit, students would listen to recordings from different jazz eras, learn about influential figures, and even try creating their own simple improvisations using classroom instruments. The unit would connect to their studies in history, culture, and even mathematics through rhythm patterns and fractions in musical time signatures.
